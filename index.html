About Me
 

Who am I?: At core…a "troubleshooter." The highlight of my day is in identifying a solution (or set of solutions) to a problem. The technology I use is just a means of fulfilling that drive but in the end, I just get a kick out of “bettering” things. As it turns out, one of the best places to do that is in Web and UX development because of all of the tools available to developers when designing, building, and analyzing their web properties. The web is where many people do their shopping, trip planning, social networking, conduct their business, and share their lives with their friends and family. Being a part of enriching those experiences is fulfilling for me.

What do I want to do?: I want to continue to build and iterate digital products; ideally, as a UX Architect/Researcher or Product Owner.

Where am I?: I am in Houston, Texas but I am open to remote/semi-remote positions in certain places where I am interested in building my retirement cabin (e.g. Tennessee, Montana, Colorado, Utah). I'm looking forward to walking 30 steps off my porch in order to catch lunch in a nearby stream...

My approach to Web Development: I believe in developing the simplest solution possible at the outset and then building up in complexity as I gain a better understanding of the needs of my userbase in my efforts to drive them down some desired conversion funnel. Bells and whistles are great but aesthetics don’t drive conversions, utility does; value does.

My approach to UX: As with most things, I approach user experience design and development by identifying the problem I am trying to solve and working my way backward from there. I strongly believe in an iterative approach where one makes small adjustments based on actual user interactions; this way I am always sure my design decisions are anchored to what the user actually needs rather than what I’m predisposed to believe about their needs. I’ve distilled my process down to the following:

Orient. Design. Build. Test. Repeat.

  • Orient myself to the problem I am trying to solve
  • Design a solution.
  • Build that solution with minimal complexity.
  • Test the solution with a small sample of my audience or using a look-a-like audience.
  • Repeat as I gather data on the efficacy of the solution.
